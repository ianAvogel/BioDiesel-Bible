Overview & Goals

This manual guides you through producing biodiesel fuel from used cooking oil. Target: $1.35 per gallon, batch size: 25–30 gallons.

Required Equipment

Show/Hide Equipment List
  • 40–50 gal tank (HDPE or stainless)
  • Heater band (1500–2000W)
  • Electric stirrer or mixer
  • Transfer pump
  • Methoxide mixing container
  • Wash tank
  • Drying system (air bubbler)

Safety Requirements

  • Wear gloves, goggles, and apron
  • Work in ventilated area
  • Store methanol in sealed metal container
  • Keep fire extinguisher nearby

Production Process

  1. Filter and dry used oil
  2. Mix methanol and lye (methoxide)
  3. Heat oil to 120°F, add methoxide while stirring
  4. Let react for 1–2 hours, then settle 8–12 hours
  5. Drain off glycerin
  6. Wash biodiesel with water, let settle
  7. Dry the fuel, then store it
